Abstract

My work sits where artificial intelligence, enterprise IT, and public policy meet — studying how nations and institutions can govern fast-moving technology without smothering it. Recent research proposes a Global Technology Governance Council and models the diffusion of generative AI markets through 2030 under competing policy regimes.

Research agenda

Three threads, one question

How do we build governance that is legitimate across borders, adaptive to technical change, and specific enough to act on? I approach it through diffusion modeling, institutional design, and risk classification.

i.

Diffusion & forecasting

Modeling generative-AI market adoption with Bass diffusion and CAGR methods across restrictive, baseline, and permissive policy regimes.

ii.

Institutional design

A proposed Global Technology Governance Council grounded in polycentric governance theory and a typology of "digital empires."

iii.

Risk classification

A Technology Risk Classification Framework for sorting emerging technologies by capability, exposure, and governance urgency.
